CHARLES DARWIN

CHARLES DARWIN

12 February 1809 – 19 April 1882

Biography

  • 1809: Born in Shrewsbury, Shropshire, England, on 12 February 1809 at his family's home, The Mount.
  • 1817: Death of his mother.
  • 1825: Beginning of his medical studies at the University of Edinburgh Medical School
  • 1828: Beginning of his theology studies at Christ's College, Cambridge to become a parson afterwards.
  • 1831: Receipt of his theology degree.
  • 1831: 1831: Voyage of the HMS Beagle begins (December, 27).
  • 1836: Return of the HMS Beagle at Falmouth, Cornwall.
  • 1839: Marriage of Emma Wedgewood.
  • 1859: Publication of On the Origin of Species
  • 1871: Publication of The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex
  • 1882: Death by heart failure on 19 April 1882.

“… If you would ask me about my heartfelt conviction, whether the nineteenth century will be called one day the iron century or the century of the steam engine or the century of the electricity, I answered without any doubt it will be called the century of the mechanistic conception of nature, the century of Darwin…”

- Ludwig Boltzmann
